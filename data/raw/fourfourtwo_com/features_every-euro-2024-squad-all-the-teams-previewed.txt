The final Euro 2024 squads haven't yet been announced, with three nations still to qualify for the tournament in Germany.Euro 2024 kicks off on June 14 with hosts Germany taking on Scotland, with the final taking place a month later on July 14 at the Olympiastadion in Berlin. FourFourTwo will cover all the news from every single fixture at Euro 2024, with expert guidance on how to watch the tournament from wherever you are in the world, too. When it comes to the squads, recent selections from countries already qualified for Euro 2024 offers a picture of what their teams might look like come the summer. Managers will likely find their preferred options in the meantime, too, as stars come in and out of form. UEFA has confirmed that Euro 2024 squads will consist of 26 players, too, after initially stating that teams will only be allowed 23 players. However, multiple nations fought against UEFA's decision to reduce the squad size, claiming that the risk of injury is greater due to extended club seasons. Following two men's tournaments where squads contained 26 players, that number will therefore remain.Each nation's boss has therefore been handed a slightly easier task in whittling down their final teams for the opening group stages, because they've got a few extra spaces to work with.UEFA has set a deadline of June 7 as the latest a nation can submit their final squads, seven days before the start of the tournament. With friendly matches taking place around this time, that'll give the majority of sides time to evaluate their players and check for any injuries. Provisional squads will likely become clear a few
